Scope of assessment

The ultimate aim of this MCI Assessment Framework is to improve the digital communications of Singapore government ministries and agencies.

The Framework does this by assessing both the strategy and implementation of digital communications for each government ministry and select statutory boards within.

The principal outcome of the assessments under the Framework is to provide recommendations for each element of the assessment to help ministries and agencies improve their digital communications.

The recommendations are not complete tutorials in how to undertake digital strategy, build a website or conduct social media engagement. Rather, they provide starting points to the assessed government entities for further research and improvement projects.

Assessment approach

The MCI Framework was developed to meet the Singapore Government's objectives for digital communications by leveraging on global digital industry best practice.

The aim is to create a fair and equitable assessment framework.

The Framework can be applied by MCI to all government ministries and agencies in Singapore. The HQ of Ministries and the agencies within them are assessed individually.

The emphasis is not on a ranking or comparison of assessed ministries, but rather on giving them insights into where to invest to improve online communications.

The MCI Framework is a pilot trial, after which, MCI will evaluate and decide on its application after it has been handed over.

Data collection

Metrics are, where possible, based on publicly available data. Strategy metrics are based on an analysis of ministries' written digital strategies. No self-assessment by ministries/agencies is required. Both qualitative and quantitative metrics are employed.

Some website metrics are based on the top 6 pages accessed on an organisation's website as well as the top 3 audiences and their top 3 scenarios, respectively, as provided by the ministry/agency.

All metrics, including qualitative measures, have been made completely numerical to facilitate the understanding of results and improve fairness.

Assessments in the purview of other organisations (e.g. accessibility as part of WIS by IDA) were not duplicated in the Framework.

Each assessment contains 227 data points. 20 entities were assessed, for a total of 4,540 data points.

Outcomes and fairness

Wherever possible, we avoided reliance on “black box” metrics provided by online tools from third party vendors to ensure independence, transparency and future flexibility of the framework.

A measurement period of 3 months (Mar – May 2014) was applied to iron out any peaks and troughs in performance, e.g. spikes due to a campaign or a new site launch.

The point of the framework is not to compare between ministries/agencies, but to provide insights and actionable recommendations.

Six rounds of fine tuning went into making sure the metrics are fair. To this end, the framework contains modifiers to account for every ministry/agency's specific circumstances.

How to read the framework results

Every metric is a score from 0-10.

A score of 10 means the ministry/agency is doing as well as can be expected based on best practice and the organisation's nature.

A low score should be a trigger for improvement efforts. Recommendations provide a starting point for these improvements.

Scores are aggregated upwards to form a tree, culminating in three top metrics for strategy, social media, and web communications.

Ministries/agencies are encouraged to drill down into branches of the tree that indicate poor performance to find where an investment of effort is most likely to yield the most improvement.
